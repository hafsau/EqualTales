# EqualTales Demo Video Script

**Total Length:** 3:30 - 4:00 minutes
**Format:** Screen recording with voiceover
**Upload:** YouTube (unlisted), captions required

---

## 1. PROBLEM STATEMENT (40 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Show a simple title card "EqualTales" or start on landing page]**

> "Every day, mothers around the world hear their children say things like 'Girls can't do math' or 'Science is for boys.' These aren't original thoughts — they're stereotypes absorbed from media, classmates, and society.

**[SCREEN: Show the example buttons grid briefly]**

> Studies show that by age six, girls already rate themselves as less talented than boys. And most parents don't have the tools or time to counter these messages effectively.

> The problem isn't that mothers don't care — it's that they don't have an easy way to respond with something more powerful than 'that's not true.'"

---

## 2. SOLUTION OVERVIEW (50 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Landing page of EqualTales]**

> "EqualTales is an AI-powered children's story engine that transforms stereotypes into personalized, illustrated bedtime stories.

**[SCREEN: Hover over the "Try It Now" button]**

> Here's how it works: A mother types the stereotype her child expressed. Our AI matches it to one of fifty real women from history who proved that stereotype wrong.

**[SCREEN: Scroll to show the stats - Age 3, Age 6, 5 Pages]**

> Then, Claude — Anthropic's most capable model — writes a five-page narrative where a fictional child discovers this real woman's story. Each page is illustrated by DALL-E 3 in a warm, watercolor children's book style.

> The result? A bedtime story that doesn't lecture — it inspires. No login required. No data stored. Just type, generate, and read together."

---

## 3. LIVE DEMO (100 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Click "Try It Now" button]**

> "Let me show you how it works."

**[SCREEN: Input form appears. Click on "Girls can't do math" example button]**

> "I'll select a common stereotype: 'Girls can't do math.' I can personalize this with my child's name and age."

**[SCREEN: Type "Sofia" in name field, adjust age slider to 5]**

> "Let's make this story for Sofia, who's five years old."

**[SCREEN: Click "Create My Story" button]**

> "Now watch the magic happen."

**[SCREEN: Generation progress screen with steps lighting up]**

> "Our system is doing several things: First, it classifies the stereotype. Then it searches our knowledge base to find the perfect historical figure. Here we go — Marie Curie, the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win in two different sciences."

**[SCREEN: Woman reveal card appears with Marie Curie]**

> "Claude is now writing a five-page story with a proven narrative arc — from belief, to question, to discovery, to inspiration, to a new belief."

**[SCREEN: Story appears, show the storybook viewer]**

> "And here's our story: 'Sofia and the Glowing Jars.'"

**[SCREEN: Click through pages slowly, pausing on illustrations]**

> "Each page has custom AI-generated illustrations. Notice how Sofia discovers Marie Curie through a magical photograph in a library. The story doesn't preach — it shows a child finding her own inspiration."

**[SCREEN: Navigate to final page]**

> "By the end, Sofia has a new belief: that math has no gender, and maybe one of the greatest mathematical minds is sitting right here."

**[SCREEN: Scroll down to companion section]**

> "Below the story, parents get discussion prompts to continue the conversation, an activity suggestion, and a QA verification badge confirming our AI checked the story for any inadvertent stereotype reinforcement."

---

## 4. IMPACT & SDG ALIGNMENT (40 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Show a simple slide or return to landing page]**

> "EqualTales directly advances three UN Sustainable Development Goals:

> SDG 4, Quality Education — by making women's history accessible to young children through engaging narratives.

> SDG 5, Gender Equality — by actively countering gender stereotypes at the age when they form most strongly.

> And SDG 10, Reduced Inequalities — by representing diverse women across STEM, sports, leadership, and the arts.

**[SCREEN: Briefly show examples grid with different stereotypes]**

> Our knowledge base includes fifty verified historical figures across fourteen stereotype categories — from Katherine Johnson calculating moon trajectories to Junko Tabei climbing Everest."

---

## 5. IDEA-THON FEEDBACK INTEGRATION (30 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Can show the app or a simple text slide]**

> "During the idea-thon, mentors gave us three key pieces of feedback:

> First, they asked about quality control. We added an AI verification step that checks every story for inadvertent bias before showing it to families.

> Second, they wanted age-appropriate content. We built age adaptations for three groups — ages 3-4, 5-7, and 8-10 — with vocabulary and complexity adjusted automatically.

> Third, they emphasized representation. We ensured our character descriptions include ten diverse appearances, randomly selected, so every child can see themselves in the story."

---

## 6. CLOSING (15 seconds)

**[SCREEN: Landing page with tagline visible]**

> "EqualTales — because representation starts at bedtime.

> Try it now at equaltales.vercel.app. Thank you."

**[SCREEN: Show URL: equaltales.vercel.app]**

## Recording Checklist

- [ ] Use QuickTime or Loom for screen recording
- [ ] Record in 1920x1080 resolution
- [ ] Speak clearly at moderate pace
- [ ] Use a cached example for faster generation (run it once before recording)
- [ ] Add captions via YouTube Studio after upload
- [ ] Set video to "Unlisted"
- [ ] Test the YouTube link works before submitting

## Backup Plan

If generation takes too long during recording:
- Pre-record the generation screen separately
- Edit clips together in iMovie
- Or use the non-streaming endpoint to pre-generate, then show the result
